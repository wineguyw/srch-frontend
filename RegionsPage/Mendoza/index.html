Mendoza

Argentina’s heart of winemaking, where high-altitude vineyards craft bold and elegant wines.

A Legacy in Every Sip

1500s - The Spanish Arrival

Spanish settlers introduced viticulture to Mendoza, bringing the first grapevines to Argentina.

1800s - The Birth of Malbec

French agronomist Michel Aimé Pouget brought Malbec to Argentina, forever changing Mendoza's wine identity.

Today - A Global Wine Powerhouse

Producing over 70% of Argentina’s wine, Mendoza is recognized worldwide for its high-altitude, high-quality wines.

The Mendoza of Today

Popular Grapes

Malbec, Cabernet Sauvignon, Bonarda, Syrah, Torrontés, Chardonnay

Acres Under Vine

Over 360,000 acres of vineyards, making it the largest wine region in Argentina.

Famous Wineries

Catena Zapata, Bodega Norton, Trapiche, Achával-Ferrer, Bodega Salentein
