Tuscany

Rolling hills, historic vineyards, and world-renowned wines define the heart of Italy.

A Legacy in Every Sip

800 BC - The Etruscans

Ancient Etruscans cultivated vineyards, establishing Tuscany as a historic wine region.

1716 - The First Wine Laws

Cosimo III de' Medici defined Chianti’s borders, making it one of the first protected wine regions.

Today - A Symbol of Italian Excellence

Tuscany’s wines, from Chianti Classico to Super Tuscans, set the global standard for fine Italian wine.

The Tuscany of Today

Popular Grapes

Sangiovese, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Vernaccia, Trebbiano, Canaiolo

Acres Under Vine

Over 150,000 acres of vineyards, home to some of Italy’s most prestigious wines.

Famous Wineries

Antinori, Biondi Santi, Castello Banfi, Frescobaldi, Tenuta San Guido (Sassicaia)
